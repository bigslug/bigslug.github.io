o hey. this is my first update! hi World!

do you ever forget everything you know along with your own identity? maybe you're along walking somewhere, or talking to your boss on the phone, or eating a slim jim, & suddenly you go completely blank? just for a moment?

above is a photo of me at Blue Lake in Alabama in 2013 with Jack (he's the dog). this was a stop on a long road trip i took with my good friend Adel. we had no real plans, only nebulous points of interest in mind, 2 weeks off work, a couple hundred dollars saved up between us, & her mom's Toyota Camry.
i've been working on an album for my band, Guests, and a song about... or rather i guess for... this place is on there. actually most of the songs on the album were written with a specific place in mind. it's kind of a concept album, but kinda loosey goosey too.
so often i listen to another artist's/band's album & i'm struck by how it is essentially a map of the emotional landscape they found themselves in at that point in time, and at the same time, a story of their passage through it. as if it could be taken in like a fantasy novel of some character's (or cast of characters') journey through strange lands, but the chapters are shuffled & the story is chopped up. and i'm left to fill in the blanks between the songs.
i wanted to write a collection of songs that took the emotional maps of my own recent experience & brought them down & overlayed them onto the actual physical maps of the world. many of the places were along my route west as i was moving my life to the other corner of the country. weirdly, i don't think it's a road album, or an album about 'america', although those things certainly are in the distant background somewhere. Despite their pitfalls, albums of that sort are something i genuinely enjoy most of the time. Bill Callahan's "Apocalypse" played nearly endlessly on that trip. i probably sang along to "America" at least once a day.
maps & my position within them were constantly on my mind, because i planned my meandering two week trip based nearly soley on free/semi-legal/no-way-this-is-legal campsites i found online, typing the raw coordinates into my map app & cross checking with my honking unwieldy road atlas. once, in the middle of the night i was lost & desperate and my friend (Adel again :) ) sent me the coordinates to an abandoned farm she knew about. in a lot of ways it was stressful & depressing to be travelling alone like that. i also felt exuberantly free at times. when i entered a house i felt strange & closterphobic. like i was too big. the world was my house.
~sean
